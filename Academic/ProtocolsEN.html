FlyPad

Food preparation

  1. Take a beaker, fill with water and heat on the heating plate.
  2. Grab the Eppendorf tubes with the food from the last draw of the freezer. They should be in the back left rack.
  3. Keep a long tweezer closed tying a rubber band at the tip. Place it on the beaker. Put in between the tweezers the Eppendorf tubes to thaw they.
  4. Stir the food with a micropipette tip if it is needed.

Flies preparation

  1. Fill an ice box with ice near to the edge.
  2. Make a hole on the ice with a fly vial.
  3. Turn side down the vial and insert it in the hole. The cotton cap should be touching the ice.
  4. Wait until the flies are completely sleep. Larger the time they are on ice, more time they will need to wake up at the device.
  5. Take out the flies to a Petri dish. Keep it on ice until you need to move the flies.

Device setting

  1. When the food is melted, grab the Eppendorf Multipette, place a new Combitip and set the dosage volume in a range between 4 and 6μL. Recommended to use 6μL.
  2. Fill the Multipette with food rising the leaver. When the leaver reaches the top, shoot one or two times on a paper towel to remove the air bubbles.
  3. Load one shoot in each cell hole. Start depositing the food deeper and rise slowly until complete the shoot. The hole must be filled completely with the food.
  4. Change Combitips between different foods. Then it is time to move the flies.
  5. Put in each cell unit of the Flypad one fly. Use a tweezer to move the flies; grab them from the wings.
  6. Close the cell lid carefully. Static electricity can stick the fly to the edge of the lid and can be smashed.
  7. Repeat until all cell units are filled. Extra flies can be toss on the ice.

Food tubes preparation

This should be done the day before to the experiment.

  1. Think about how much beakers you will need depending on the number of tubes to use. Small tubes should contain 10mL of food. That is approximately 1 or 2cm of the tube.
  2. Pour inside the beaker the food from the food vials. Help you with a scale larger spoon.
  3. Heat the beaker in the microwave for short periods of time. Be careful to not allow food to boil.
  4. Left beakers on the bench and let them to cool down. To check if it is ready, place a hand over the beaker, if you feel the heat and humidity then it is not ready yet.
  5. Use micropipette tip to stir the food and accelerate the cool down process.
  6. Meanwhile the food is reaching the temperature, prepare the small tubes. Grab 16 tubes, put them together and hold them tying a rubber band around them. Depending on the size of the beaker, it could store more tube packs.
  7. When the food is cold it is the time to add drugs or another stuff. If not, move to the next step.
  8. Place the tube packs inside the beaker straight up. Level the tubes that everyone touches the bottom of the beaker. All of them should keep themselves straight up.
  9. Let them on a bench or a shelf to solidify the food. If you prepare the food around 2pm you can leave on the bench and put the caps the next day.
